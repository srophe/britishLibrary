Add. 14,607

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/343
Description based on Wright DCCXLVII (2:683-684)
Date: 6th or 7th century
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 121 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Collected Authors
This manuscript contains: John Chrysostom. Discourse of John Chrysostom on Psalm li.; Jacob of Batnae. Metrical discourses of Jacob of Batnae; Isaac of Antioch. Metrical discourses of Isaac of Antioch; Basil. Two letters of Basil to a fallen virgin; viz.; Jacob of Batnae. Letter of Jacob of Batnae to two harlots, who repented and became recluses; .... In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 19 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 10 in. by 7 1/8, consisting of 121 leaves, some of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 1, 11, 25, and 120. The quires, signed with letters, were originally 16 in number, but of these ܗ and ܘ are lost, and ܐ ,ܕ and ܝܘ are very imperfect. They have been subsequently numbered from ܐ to ܝܓ. Leaves are wanting both at the beginning and end, as well as after foll. 5 and 30. Each page is divided into two columns, of from 20 to 29 lines.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This volume is written in a clear, though rather inelegant hand of the vith or viith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Greek and Syriac vowels have been occasionally added at a later period.
