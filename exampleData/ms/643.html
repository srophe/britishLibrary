Add. 14,733

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/643
Description based on Wright DCCCCLXI (3:1139-1140)
Date: AD 1199 / AG 1510
Origin: Convent of S. Mary Deipara, in the desert of Scete.
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Paper Codex, 94 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Collected Lives
This manuscript contains: Zakhe. Histories of Saints and Martyrs. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 8 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 8 1/8 in. by 5 1/2, consisting of 94 leaves, some of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 71 and 79—94. The quires, signed with letters, are 11 in number. Leaves are wanting after foll. 79, 81, 90, and at the end of the volume. There are from 17 to 24 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a good, regular hand, and dated A. Gr. 1510, A.D. 1199.

Notes:
  • On fol. 32 b there is a note, stating that the manuscript was written in the convent of S. Mary Deipara, in the desert of Scete, A. Gr. 1510 (A.D. 1199), by a monk from the convent of Mar Matthew, named Zakhe, a native of a place called ܟܪ̈ܡܐ ܩܸܣܛܪܐ, near Tagrit.
  • We read a similar note on f. 52a.
