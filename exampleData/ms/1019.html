Add. 17,234

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1019
Description based on Wright CCCCVII (1:317-318)
Date: 13th century
Melkite Syriac script ܀ Paper Codex, 150 leaves.
Features: Decoration
Wright's Subject Classification: Choral Books
This manuscript contains: John of Damascus ܐܒܐ ܝܘܐܢܝ ܕܕܪܘܡܣܘܩ, Cosmas ܐܒܢ ܩܘܙܡܐ (ܩܘܣܡܐ), Andrew (of Crete) ܚܣܝܐ ܐܢܕܪܘܣ (ܐܢܕܐܪܘܣ) ,, Joseph ܐܒܐ ܝܘܣܦ, Mark ܐܒܐ ܡܪܩܘܣ, Theophanes, ܚܣܝܐ ܬܘܦܢܘܣ. The second part of the Triôdion. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 18 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 10 1/4 in. by 6 7/8, consisting of 150 leaves, a few of which are much torn, especially foll. 1, 6, and 13. The quires, signed with letters, were originally 21 in number; but ܐ and ܟܐ are lost, and ܒ and ܟ are very imperfect, leaves being wanting after foll. 1, 5, and 149. There are from 19 to 21 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (sole, Melkite Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a good, regular, Malkite hand of the xiiith cent.
Decoration (Marginal): There are some rude attempts at ornamentation on the margins.
