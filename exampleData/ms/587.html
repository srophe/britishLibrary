Add. 14,715, foll. 1–152

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/588
Description based on Wright CCCCLXXXVII (1:379-380)
Date: AD 1257 / AG 1568
Origin: Convent of S. Mary Deipara
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Paper Codex, 152 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Hymns
This manuscript contains: A collection of Common Supplications, arranged according to the eight tones; Various supplications in irregular sequence. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 22 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 8 1/8 in. by 5 1/2, consisting of 152 leaves, some of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 1 and 146. The quires, signed with Syriac, and by a later hand with Armenian letters, are 16 in number. A leaf is wanting at the beginning, and there is a lacuna after fol. 149. There are 15 or 16 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a large, regular hand; dated A. Gr. 1568, A.D. 1257.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Armenian script): A later hand has signed the quires with Armenian letters.

Add. 14,715, foll. 153–216

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/589
Description based on Wright CCC (1:233-234)
Date: 13th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Paper Codex, 64 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Sacerdotals
This manuscript contains: The Order of the Consecration of Water; Jacob of Edessa. A letter of Jacob of Edessa to the priest Addai regarding the Orders of Baptism and of the Consecration of Water on the night of the Epiphany; The Order of the Washing of Feet; A prayer to be substituted on the Thursday in Passion week, at the commencement of the Eucharistic service, for the oratio pacis; The Order of Genuflexion on Pentecost, in three parts; .... In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 11 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 8 1/8 in. by 5 5/8, consisting of 64 leaves (Add. 14,715, foll. 153—216), the first 7 of which are much stained and torn. The quires, signed with letters, are 7 in number. Leaves are wanting at the beginning and after fol. 158. There are from 14 to 22 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a good, regular hand of the xiiith cent.
