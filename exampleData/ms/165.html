Add. 14,480, foll. 1–61, 63–120, 122–128

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1179
Date: 5th or 6th century
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 128 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: New Testament
This manuscript contains: The Epistles of S. Paul, according to the Peshitta version. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 15 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 9 7/8 in. by 7 7/8, consisting of 128 leaves, some of which are slightly stained and torn, especially foll. 1, 2, 7, 121, 127, and 128. The quires are now signed with letters up to ܝܘ, but what the original signatures were does not appear; they seem to have been also numbered in the reverse way with Greek letters at the top (foll. 31 b, ΙΒ; 22 b, ΙΓ). Each page is divided into two columns, of from 21 to 26 lines. Fol. 62 is a palimpsest leaf of the ixth or xth cent., written by the same hand that repaired fol. 121 (which is also partly palimpsest). See Add. MS 14,480 foll. 62, 121, upper text and Add. MS 14,480 foll. 62, 121, lower text

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): This volume is written in a large, regular Estrangela of the 5th or 6th century.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Greek script): See additions.
Hand 4 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 5 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.

Hand 7 (minor, West Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 8 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 9 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 10 (minor, Unspecified Greek script): See additions.

Additions

Addition 2: The quires seem to have been also numbered in the reverse way with Greek letters at the top (foll.31 b, ΙΒ;22 b, ΙΓ).
Addition 3: Foll.1—10, 12, 13, and 47 are from a manuscript of nearly the same date.
Addition 4: Fol. 62 is a palimpsest leaf of the ixth or xth cent., written by the same hand that repaired fol.121(which is also partly palimpsest).
Addition 5: Foll.11, 14, 29, 30, 40-46, and 55 are paper leaves of the xiiith cent.
Addition 6: Greek vowels are but rarely added.
Addition 7: A few lessons have, been noted by a modern hand.
Addition 8 (Fol. 1a): On fol. 1 a there is a note, apprising us that this was one of the volumes brought to the convent of S. Mary Deipara by the abbat Moses of Nisibis, ܡܘܫܐ ܪܝܫܕܝܪܐ ܢܨܝܒܢܝܐ
