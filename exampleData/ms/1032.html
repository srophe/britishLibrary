Add. 17,243, foll. 1–5, 7–174

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1422
Description based on Wright CCCXLV (1:290-291)
Date: 13th century
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Paper Codex, 174 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Choral Books
This manuscript contains: Greek and Syriac canons for the first half of the year. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 20 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 7 in. by 5 ¼, consisting of 174 leaves. The quires are without signatures. There are from 13 to 17 lines in each page

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This volume is written in a rather inelegant hand of the xiiith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.

Additions

N.B., folio location may not be available for some items below.
Addition 1: The name of the scribe Yeshūa' (bar Phetion) appears on fol. 125 a and fol. 142a ܨܠܘ ܥܠ ܕܣ̣ܪܛ ܢܬܚܢܢ ܚܛܝܐ ܝܫܘܥ ܒܝܘܡ ܕܝ̣ܢܐ ܕܚܝܻܠܐ ܘܙܝܻ̇ܥܐ
Addition 2 (Fol. 1): Foll. 1 and 2 contain a list of Syriac words, arranged alphabetically in columns from ܛ to ܢܢ.
Addition 3: Foll. 3, 4, 5, and 174, exhibit mere trials of the pen in Syriac and Coptic.
Addition 4: Foll. 7 and 172 are covered with columns of Coptic arithmetical figures; and fol. 173 b contains two or three lines of Coptic writing.
