Add. 14,451*

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/123
Description based on Wright LXXXVIII (1:62-63)
Date: 8th century
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 61 leaves.
Features: Decoration
Wright's Subject Classification: New Testament
This manuscript contains: The first three Gospels according to the Peshitta version. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 4 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 12 in. by 9 1/2, consisting of 61 leaves, some of which are slightly stained and torn, especially foll. 37 and 61. The quires are signed with letters (ܚ, fol. 38 a; ܛ, fol. 48 a), but their number is uncertain, owing to the defective state of the volume. Leaves are wanting at the beginning and end, as well as after foll. 37, 49, and 51. Each page is divided into two columns, of from 27 to 33 lines. See no. LXXIV , with which a portion of this manuscript has been bound, viz. Add. 14,452, foll. 1-22 .

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): This volume is written in a fine, regular Estrangela of the viiith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Foll. 9—18 may be of the xth cent.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Numerous Syriac vowels and diacritical marks have been added by a still later hand.
Decoration (Rubrication): Lessons are rubricated in the text throughout the volume.

Manuscript Contents

Note: Item numbering updated in digital edition.
Item 1 (Fol. 1a): The first three Gospels according to the Peshitta version [defective]
Note: The Ammonian sections and Eusebian canons are marked in the text with red ink, and there is a harmony at the foot of each page (except foll. 9—18).
Item 2 (Fol. 1a): S. Matthew, commencing with ch. xxiii. v. 28 [defective]
Note: A considerable part of the missing portion, viz. ch. ix. 7—xxiii. 28, has been long bound up with Add. 14,452 . See no. LXXIV , Add. 14,452, foll. 1-22 .
Item 3 (Fol. 10b): S. Mark
Item 4 (Fol. 34b): S. Luke [defective]
Note: The missing portions are: ch. ii. 37—iii. 12, ix. 44—x. 8, xi. 13— 37, and xviii. 20 to the end.
