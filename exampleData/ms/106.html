Add. 14,442, foll. 2–46

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1506
Description based on Wright XLVIII (1:28-29)
Date: 7th century
Estrangela script ܀ Parchment Codex, 45 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Old Testament
This manuscript contains: The book of Genesis, translated from the Septuagint by Paul, bishop of Tellā or Constantina .See Monumenta Sacra et Profana opera Collegii Doctorum Bibl. Ambros., tom. i. fasc. i. Prolegomena in editionem versionis Syriacae ex textu LXX., p. iii. “ Paulus Episcopus Monophysita Telae Mauslath, aliorum fortasse opera adjustus, jussu Athanasii Patriarchae Monophysitae Antiocheni vertit LXX. in Syriacum annis 616-7 Chr., fortasse etiam sequentibus, in urbe Alexandriae.” Most of the books of this version have already been edited by Norberg, Bugati, Middledorpf, and Rördam; and an edition of the whole is now being printed by Dr. Ceriani of Milan in the work cited above. He has given a minute description of this manuscript of the book of Genesis in tom. ii. fasc. ii. p. xvii., etc. See also his memoir, Le Edizioni e i Maoscritti etc., p. 19. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 3 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 3/4 in. by 5 3/8, consisting of 45 leaves, many of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 3 — 6, 9, 10, 17 — 21, 35, and 46. The quires, signed with letters, were originally 13 in number, but several are now lost, and only ܓ, ܝـ, and ܝܒ, are complete. Leaves are wanting after foll. 2, 6, 8, 18, 19, 20, 21, 33, and 45. Each page has from 22 to 28 lines.

Hand 1 (sole, Estrangela script): This manuscript is written in a good, regular Estrangela of the viith cent.

Note: The following passages of the text are missing: ch. i. 1—iv. 8, ix. 24— xvi. 2, xvi. 12 — xx. 1, xx. 13 — xxxi. 53, xxxii. 12 — xxxvi. 2, xl. 18 — xliii. 1, and xlvii. 16—1. 17. The lessons are rubricated in the text, the end of each being indicated by ܫ. In the ܩ̈ܦܠܐܐ they are noted on the margins. The text exhibits the critical marks of the Hexapla of Origen; and the margins contain the various readings of Aquila (ܐ), Symmachus (ܣ), and Theodotion (ܬ); glosses upon some words (e.g. foll. 24 a, 25 a, 27 b, 29 a, 30 a, etc.); and a good many Greek vocables, written in not inelegant characters. The tetragrammaton is expressed by ܦܝܦܝ.

Add. 14,442, foll. 47–65

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/108
Description based on Wright XXIII (1:15)
Date: 6th or 7th century
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 19 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Old Testament
This manuscript contains: Part of the first book of Samuel, according to the Peshitta version. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 1 item.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 1/4 in. by 5 3/8, consisting of 19 leaves (Add. 14,442, fol. 47—65) one of which (fol. 55) is much stained. Leaves are wanting after fol. 54 and at the end. Each page has from 19 to 21 lines. This manuscript is written in a fine, regular Estrangela of the vith or viith cent. A few Greek vowels have been added by a later hand on fol. 48band 49a(%).

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): This manuscript is written in a fine, regular Estrangela of the vith or viith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, West Syriac script): A few Greek vowels have been added by a later hand.See additions.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 4 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See below.
Hand 5 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See below.
Decoration (Rubrication):

Contents

This manuscript contains 1 item(s). N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 47): Part of the first book of Samuel, according to the Peshitta version. [defective]
 
Title: ܢܒܝܘܬܗ ܕܫܡܘܐܝܠ  
Title: ܩܕܡܝܬܐ ܕܫܡܘܐܝܠ
Notes:
  • The missing portions of the text are: ch. ii. 29—xvii. 57, and ch. xx. 34 to the end.
  • Of the lessons, some are rubricated in the text, and others are marked by a later hand on the margin.

Additions

N.B., items below may have been listed out of folio order in Wright's catalogue.
Addition 1 (Fol. 48b): A few Greek vowels have been added by a later hand on fol. 48b and 49a.
Addition 2 (Fol. 48a): On fol. 48a there is a note, recording that this book belonged to the brothers Matthew, Abraham and Theodore of Tagrīt: ܐܝܬܘܗܝ ܟܬܒܐ ܗܢܐ ܕܡܬܝ ܘܐܒܪܗܡ ܘܬܐܕܪܘܣ ܐܚ̈ܐ ܬܓܪ̈ܝܬܢܝܐ. ܙܒܢܘܗܝ ܐܝܟ ܕܠܝܘܬܪܢܐ ܕܚܝܝ̈ܗܘܢ ܘܟܠ ܕܦܓܥ ܒܗ. ܟܠ ܕܝܢ ܕܠܚܐ ܠܥܘܕܢܗ̈ܘܢ (sic) ܡܢ ܟܬܒܐ ܗܢܐ. ܢܗܘܐ ܚܪܡ ܡܢ ܬܠܝܬܝܘܬܐ ܩܕܝܫܬܐ. ܘܫܘܝܬ ܒܐܘܣܝܐ ܐܝܢ ܘܐܡܝܢ.
Addition 3 (Fol. 48a): Just above this, there is another note, stating that in the year 1517 of the Greeks, A.D. 1206, twelve brethren from Syria entered the convent of S. Mary Deipara: ܒܫܢܬ ܐ̄ ܘܚܡܫܡܐܐ ܘܝܙ̄ ܕܝܘܢܝ̈ܐ ܒܢܝܣ̇ܢ ܝܪܚܐ ܥܠܢܢ ܠܕܝܪܐ ܕܣܘܪ̈ܝܝܐ ܕܒܡܕܒܪܐ ܕܐܣܩܛܝ ܬܪ̈ܥܣܪ ܐܚ̈ܐ ܕܡܢ ܣܘܪܝܐ.
Addition 4 (Fol. 48a): Of the lessons, some are rubricated in the text, and others are marked by a later hand on the margin.
