URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/624
Description based on Wright DCCCXLII (2:882-883)
Date: 13th century
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Paper Codex, 75 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Collected Authors
This manuscript contains: Evagrius. Extracts from the writings of Evagrius; John the Monk. Discourse of John the monk on S. Matthew, ch. v. 3; Macarius. Letters of Macarius; A discourse extracted from the Climax or Ladder; The history of Marcus the solitary, who dwelt on the hill of Tharmaka; .... In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 16 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 6 7/8 in. by 5, consisting of 75 leaves, some of which are much soiled and torn, especially foll. 1—7 and 75. The quires, signed with letters, are 7 in number. Leaves are wanting at the beginning, and after foll. 1, 4, 5, and 71. Each page has from 16 to 26 lines.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written, apparently by two persons, in a current hand of the xiiith cent.

Paper, about 6 1/2 in. by 5, consisting of 31 leaves (Add. 14,728, foll. 208—238), many of which are slightly stained and torn. The quires, signed with letters, are three in number. There are from 20 to 30 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a current hand of the xiith or xiiith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): The writing on the flyleaf, fol. 208, and on fol. 209 a, is different.
