Add. 17,197, foll. 1–25

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/854
Description based on Wright DLXII (2:440-441)
Date: 9th or 10th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Codex, 25 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Individual Authors
This manuscript contains: A Commentary on the first volume of the Works of Gregory Nazianzen , as translated by the abbat Paul, compiled by an anonymous author, who followed the exposition of Benjamin, metropolitan of Edessa. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 4 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 9 in. by 6, consisting of 25 leaves, some of which are much soiled, especially foll. 1—7. The quires are signed with letters. There are from 26 to 31 lines in each page. This manuscript is imperfect at the end, and there are lacunae after foll. 3, 9, and 12.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a small, regular hand of the ixth or xth cent.

Contents

This manuscript contains 4 item(s) including nested subsections. N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Add. 17,197, foll. 26–46

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/855
Description based on Wright DCCXI (2:606-608)
Date: 9th or 10th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Codex, 21 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Individual Authors
This manuscript contains: Portions of a monophysite treatise on the doctrine of the Two Natures in Christ. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 7 items.

Physical Description

Some leaves are much stained and soiled, especially foll.28—33, 39, and 43.

This manuscript is imperfect both at the beginning and end, and there are lacunae after foll.26, 27, 33, 43, and 44.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): It is written in a good, regular hand of the ixth or xth cent.

Contents

This manuscript contains 7 item(s) including nested subsections. N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 26a): Portions of a monophysite treatise on the doctrine of the Two Natures in Christ. [defective]
Notes:
  • As it is directed against Leo, bishop of Harrān, and is divided into twelve chapters, there can be no doubt that it is the "Epistola Apologetica ad Leonem" of Elias, patriarch of Antioch, described by Assemani in the Bibl. Or., t. ii., p. 95.
  • Chapp. i.—vi. are wanting.
  • The principal writers cited are:

    Acacius of Melitene:fol. 33 b.
