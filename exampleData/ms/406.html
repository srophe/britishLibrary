Add. 14,642, upper text

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1514
Description based on Wright DCCCCXVI (3:1041-1042)
Date: early 10th century
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 41 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: History
This manuscript contains: Part of a Chronicle, chiefly ecclesiastical. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 3 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 9 1/8 in. by 6, consisting of 41 leaves, many of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 1—8, 12, 22, 24, 25, 31, 32, and 34—41. The quires, signed with letters, seem to have been 5 in number, but only ܓ is perfect, leaves being wanting at the beginning, and after foll. 7, 13, 23, and 31. There are from 30 to 34 lines in each page. This volume is palimpsest throughout, being made up, to all appearance, of portions of five Greek manuscripts.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This volume is written in a good, regular hand of the earlier part of the xth cent., with the exception of foll. 36 b—39 a, which are in a smaller and more cursive character of about the same date.

Contents

This manuscript contains 3 item(s) including nested subsections. N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 1b): Part of a Chronicle, chiefly ecclesiastical [defective]
Note: Foll. 40 and 41 are small fragments, belonging to the older part of the volume, but of which it seems impossible to determine the proper place.
Item 2 (Fol. 1b): Part of a Chronicle, chiefly ecclesiastical, compiled from the similar works of Eusebius, fol. 1 b, Andronicus, foll. 1 b, 15 a, and others, and continued to A. Gr. 1108, A.D. 797, fol. 36 a. [defective]
Item 3 (Fol. 36b): Later additions that bring the history down to A.Gr. 1122, A.D. 811 [defective]

Add. 14,642, foll. 17, 20, 24–25, 27–29, 31–32, 34–40, lower text

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1515
Description based on Wright (3:1041-1042)
Date: early 9th century
Unspecified Greek script ܀ Parchment Codex, 11 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification:
This manuscript contains: Catena Patrum. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 3 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 9 1/8 in. by 6. Foll. 17, 20, 24, 25, 27—29, 31, 32, and 34—40, seem to belong to a Catena Patrum.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Greek script): These leaves are written in slanting uncials of the earlier half of the ixth cent. The character of the rubrics is larger and more upright.
