Add. 17,160, foll. 1–18

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/798
Description based on Wright DCL (2:512-13)
Date: 7th century
Estrangela script ܀ Parchment Codex, 18 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification:
This manuscript contains: Jacob of Batnae. Metrical discourses ofJacobofBatnae. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 5 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, 8 3/4 in. by 5 3/8, consisting of 18 leaves, written in a good, regular hand of the viith cent., with from 23 to 25 lines in each page. There is a considerable lacuna after fol. 10.

Hand 1 (sole, Estrangela script): This manuscript is written in a good, regular hand of the viith cent.

Contents

This manuscript contains 5 item(s) including nested subsections. N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 1): Jacob of Batnae. Metrical discourses of Jacob of Batnae [defective]
Item 2 (Fol. 1a): Jacob of Batnae. The fourth discourse on the End of the World [defective]
 
Title: ܡܐܡܪܐ ܕܐܪ̈ܒܥܐ ܕܥܠ ܚܪܬܐ. ܕܐܡܝܪ ܠܡܪܝ ܝܥܩܘܒ ܡܠܦܢܐ ܕܫܪܪܐ.
Note: A small portion of the conclusion alone remains.
Item 3 (Fol. 1b): Jacob of Batnae. On Drunkards
 
Title: ܡܐܡܪܐ ܕܥܠ ܪ̈ܘܝܐ   Incipit: ܡܠܦܢܘܬܐ ܐܝܟ ܐܣܝܬܐ ܗܝ ܠܕܫ̇ܡܥ ܠܗ̇. ܪ̈ܚܡܝ ܚ̈ܝܐ ܠܐ ܬܫܘܛܘܢܗ̇ ܠܡܫܒܚܬܐ. ܏ܘܫ..
Item 4 (Fol. 11a): Jacob of Batnae. On the Youth who asked our Lord, "What shall I do that I may inherit eternal life? [defective]
 
Title: ܡܐܡܪܐ ܕܥܠ ܗ̇ܘ ܥܠܝܡܐ ܕܐܦܝܣ ܠܡܪܢ. ܕܡܢܐ ܐܥܒܕ ܕܐܝܪܬ ܚܝ̈ܐ ܕܠܥܠܡ..
Note: Only the subscription remains.
Item 5 (Fol. 11a): Jacob of Batnae. That the Lawgiver of the Old and New Testaments is one
 
Title: ܡܐܡܪܐ ܕܡܚܘܐ ܕܚܕ ܗܘ ܣܐܡ ܢܡܘܣܐ̇. ܕܥܬܝܩܬܐ ܘܕܚܕܬܐ,   Incipit: ܐܕܝܠ ܒܝ ܡܪܝ ܡܠܬܐ ܕܡܠܝܐ ܟܠ ܝܘܬܪ̈ܢܐ. ܘܙܡܪܐ ܫܘܒܚܟ ܒܟܢܘ̈ܫܬܟ ܥܬܝܪܐܝܬ.
Note: See Assemani, Bibl. Or., t. i., p. 319, no. 102.

Add. 17,160, foll. 19–23

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/799
Description based on Wright DCIV (2:481-482)
Date: 6th or 7th century
Estrangela script ܀ Parchment Other, 5 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification:
This manuscript contains: Mark the Monk. Portions of the discourse of Marcus the monk on the Spiritual Law. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 1 item.

Physical Description

Five vellum leaves, about 8 5/8 in. by 5 3/8, all more or less stained and the last much mutilated (Add. 17,160, foll. 19—23). Each page is divided into two columns of 29 or 30 lines.

Hand 1 (sole, Estrangela script): The writing is a good, regular Estrangělā of the vith or viith cent.

Contents

This manuscript contains 1 item(s). N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 19a): Mark the Monk. Portions of the discourse of Marcus the monk on the Spiritual Law. [defective]
Note: Cf. Assemani, Bibl. Or., t. iii., pars 1, p. 45; Gallandii Bibl. Vett. Patrum, t. viii., p. 1; Migne, Patrol. Gr., t. lxv., col. 905

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/800
Description based on Wright DCCCLXIX (2:1018)
Date: ca. 8th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Other, 3 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Anonymous Works
This manuscript contains: Part of a discourse on S. John the Baptist (S. Matthew, ch. iii.); Part of a discourse on the Resurrection of our Lord. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 2 items.

Three vellum leaves, about 8 5/8 in. by 5 1/4, the second and third of which are much torn (Add. 17,160, foll. 24—26). Each page is divided into two columns, of from 28 to 36 lines.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): The writing is good and regular, of about the viiith cent.
