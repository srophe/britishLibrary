Add. 14,630, foll. 1–27

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/375
Description based on Wright DCCCX (2:818-819)
Date: 10th century
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 27 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Collected Authors
This manuscript contains: Palladius. Anecdotes of the Egyptian Fathers from the work of Palladius; Ephraim. Metrical discourses of Ephraim; Jacob of Batnae. Metrical discourses of Jacob of Batnae. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 10 items.

Physical Description

Vellum about 7 7/8 in. by 6, consisting of 27 leaves, some of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 1, 11, 12, and 15—17. The quires are signed with letters, but only two of them are complete, ܛ and ܝܙ. A later hand has marked them with Greek letters, Θ and ΙΖ. Leaves are wanting at the beginning and end, as well as after foll. 10, 11, 12, 14, 15, and 25. There are from 19 to 23 lines in each page. This manuscript is written in a good, regular hand of the xth cent.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a good, regular hand of the xth cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): The quires are signed with letters, but only two of them are complete,ܛ and ܝܙ.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Greek script): A later hand has marked them with Greek letters,Θ and ΙΖ.

Add. 14,630, foll. 29–41

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/377
Description based on Wright DCIII (2:481)
Date: 9th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Codex, 13 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Individual Authors
This manuscript contains: John Chrysostom, John IV, patriarch of Constantinople. The discourse ofJohn Chrysostomon Virginity and Repentance. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 1 item.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 3/8 in. by 6 1/8, consisting of 13 leaves (Add. 14,630, foll. 29—41), all slightly stained and torn, especially foll. 29—32, 38, and 41. Each page is divided into two columns, of from 33 to 38 lines. This manuscript is written in a neat hand of the ixth cent.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a neat hand of the ixth cent.

Contents

This manuscript contains 1 item(s). N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 29a): John Chrysostom, John IV, patriarch of Constantinople. The discourse of John Chrysostom on Virginity and Repentance [defective]
 
Title: ܡܐܡܪܐ ܕܩܕܝܫܐ ܡܪܝ ܝܘܐܢܝܣ ܕܥܠ ܒܬ܏ܘܠ ܘܬܝܒܘܬܐ ܘܡܪܬܝܢܘܬܐ   Colophon: See below.
Notes:
  • The text is rather of John IV, called ὁ Νηστευτής(Jejunator, or the Faster), patriarch of Constantinople, A.D. 582—595
  • Imperfect at the beginning.
  • Cf. Le Quien, Or. Christ., t. i., col. 226; the Works of Chrysostom, ed. Saville, t. vii., p. 641.

Additions

Addition 1 (Fol. 41b): A note at the end, in the handwriting of the scribe, states that the manuscript was written for the monk Joseph, of the place called "little Baddāyā,"ܒ̇ܕܝܐ ܙܥܘܪܬܐ, near Harrān
