Add. 18,819, foll. 1–91

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1128
Description based on Wright CCCCXXIV (1:340-341)
Date: 9th or 10th century
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 91 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Hymns
This manuscript contains: Severus, John bar Aphtunaya. The Hymns of Severus, John bar Aphtūnāyā, etc. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 14 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 5 1/8 in. by 3 7/8, consisting of 91 leaves, many of which are much stained and soiled. The quires are signed with letters; but several are missing at the beginning and end, and there is a lacuna after fol. 8. Some other lacunae have been supplied by a later hand. The number of lines in each page varies from 18 to 22.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): The greater part of this manuscript is written in a small, neat hand of the ixth or xth cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Foll. 42—44, 49, and 80—91 are the exception, which are of the xiith or xiiith cent.

Add. 18,819, foll. 92–109

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1129
Description based on Wright CCCCXXIII (1:340)
Date: AD 884 / AG 1195
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Other, 18 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Hymns
This manuscript contains: Severus, John bar Aphtūnāyā. The Hymns of Severus, John bar Aphtūnāyā, etc., from no. 374 (ܫܥܕ) to no. 402 (ܬܒ); Anthems to be chanted before the administration of the holy Eucharist; Prosphorici for several occasions. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 5 items.

Physical Description

Eighteen vellum leaves, about 5 1/2 in. by 3 7/8, the last two quires (ܝܗ and ܝܘ) of a manuscript (Add. 18,819, foll. 92—109). There are from 17 to 21 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): The writing is neat and regular. These vellum leaves are dated A. Gr. 1195, A.D. 884.

Contents

This manuscript contains 5 item(s) including nested subsections. N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 92a): Severus, John bar Aphtūnāyā. The Hymns of Severus, John bar Aphtūnāyā, etc., from no. 374 (ܫܥܕ) to no. 402 (ܬܒ)
  Subscription (Fol. 101b): ܫܠܡ ܠܡܟܬܒ ܡ̈ܥܢܝܬܐ ܕܩܕܝܫܐ ܘܠܒ̣ܝܫ ܠܐܠܗܐ ܡܪܝ ܣܐܘܪܐ ܦܛܪܝܪܟܐ ܕܟܘܪܣܝܐ ܫܠܝܚܝܐ ܕܐܢܛܘܝܟܝܐ (sic) ܡܕܝܢܬܐ ܕܣܘܪܝܐ
Item 2 (Fol. 92a):
 
Title: ܡ̈ܥܢܝܬܐ ܕܥ̈ܢܝܕܐ
Note: From no. 8 to no. 26.
Item 3 (Fol. 98b):
 
Title: ܛܟܣܐ ܕܡ̈ܥܢܝܬܐ ܐܘܟܝܬ ܡܬܟ̈ܫܦܢܘܬܐ ܕܡܬܡܪܢ ܒܙܒܢܐ ܕܪܘܓܙܐ ܘܫܒܛܐ ܕܡܪܕܘܬܐ ܕܡܬܝܬܝܢ ܥܠ ܒܢܝ̈ܢܫܐ
Note: These are 11 in number
Item 4 (Fol. 101b): Anthems to be chanted before the administration of the holy Eucharist
 
Title: ܬܘܒ ܐܢܛܝ̈ܦܘܢܐ ܐܘܟܝܬ ܦܬܓ̈ܡܐ ܕܡܬܐܡܪ̈ܝܢ ܡܢ ܟܗ̈ܢܐ. ܘܥ̇ܢܐ ܥܡܐ ܒܬܪܗܘܢ ܡܢ ܩܕܡ ܕܡܬܝ̈ܗܒܝܢ ܪ̈ܐܙܐ ܩ̈ܕܝܫܐ ܠܥܡܐ
Item 5 (Fol. 102a): Prosphorici for several occasions
 
Title: ܦܪܣܦܪܝܩܘ ܕܡܬܐܡܪ̈ܝܢ ܟܕ ܡ̈ܬܝܗܒܢ (sic)ܪ̈ܐܙܐ ܩ̈ܕܝܫܐ  Subscription (Fol. 108a): ܐܫܬ̣ܡܠܝܬ݁ ܦܢܩܝܬܐ ܗܕܐ ܕܡ̈ܥܢܝܬܐ ܕܩܕܝܫܐ ܡܪܝ ܣܐܘܪܐ ܦܐܛܪܝܪܟܐ ܕܐܢܛܝܘܟܝܐ ܕܣܘܪܝܐ ܫܢܬ ܐܠܦ ܘܡܐܐ ܘܬܫ̈ܥܝܢ ܘܚܡܫ ܕܐܠܟܣܢܕܪܘܣ ܒܪ ܦܝܠܝܦܘܣ ܡܐܩܕܘܢܝܐ.
Note: The subscription gives the date.

Additions

Addition 1 (Fol. 108a): On fol. 108 a, there is a note, stating that the book was written and bound by a man from the village of Gadmin. His name has, however, been erased, and that of Simeon substituted. ܐܬܚ̇ܦܛ ܘܩ̣ܢܐ ܐܘܿܟܝܬ ܟܬ̣ܒ ܘܕܒ̇ܩ ܣܝܡܬܐ ܗܕܐ ܪܘܚܢܝܬܐ [ܫܡܥܘܢ] ܡܚܝܠܐ ܘܐܟܣܢܝܐ ܕܡܢ ܓܕܡܝܢ ܩܪܝܬܐ ܐܝܟ ܚܝܠܐ ܕܝܠܗ ܏ܘܫ.
Addition 2 (Fol. 109a): Another note, on fol. 109 a, gives the name of the person for whom it was written, now altered into Daniel ܒܚܦܝܛܘܬܐ ܕܡܛܠ ܐܠܗܐ ܐܝܨܦ ܘܩ̣ܢܐ ܣܚܚܐ ܗܢܐ ܐܝܟ ܕܠܝܘܬܪܢܐ ܕܢܦܫܗ ܘܝܘܬܪܢܐ ܕܟܠ ܕܦ̇ܓܥ ܒܗ. [ܕܢܝـ]ܐܝܠ ܕܝܪܝܐ ܐܘܟܝܬ ܐܟܣܢܝܐ. ܕܐܠܗܐ ܗ̇ܘ ܕܡܛܠ ܫܡܗ ܐܝܨܦ ܘܙܒ̣ܢ ܗ̣ܘ ܢܥܒܕ ܕܘܟܪܢܐ ܠܥܢ̣̈ܝܕܘܗܝ ܒܩܝܡܐ ܕܙܕܝܩܐ. ܒܨ̈ܠܘܬܐ ܕܟܠܗܘܢ ܡܢܝ̈ܚܢܐ ܕܡܫܝܚܐ. ܝܬܝܪܐܝܬ ܕܡܪܬܐ ܕܝܠܢ ܡܪܝܡ ܐܝܢ ܘܐܡܝܢ.

Description based on Wright CCCCXXVIII (1:345)
Date: 10th and 11th centuries
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Other, 11 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Hymns
This manuscript contains: Severus. Part of the Hymns of Severus. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 5 items.

Physical Description

Eleven vellum leaves, about 5 3/8 in. by 3 3/4, the last four of which are much torn (Add. 18,819, foll. 110—120). These vellum leaves are fragments of two manuscripts of the xth and xith centuries, which have been joined together by the later scribe.
