Three vellum leaves, about 8 in. by 5 7/8, slightly stained and soiled. They belonged to a Malkite Octôechus, written in a good, regular hand of the xith cent., with 17 or 18 lines in each page.

Add. 14,522, foll. 4–26

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/218
Description based on Wright CCCIX (1:246-247)
Date: AD 818-888 / AG 1129-1199
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 23 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Choral Books
This manuscript contains: Hymns for the festivals of the whole year. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 16 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 in. by 6 1/8, consisting of 23 leaves (Add. 14,522, foll. 4—26), many of which are much stained and torn, especially foll.5, 10, 11, 19, 20, 22—26. Leaves are wanting at the beginning, and after foll. 11, 19 and 21. The quires are signed with letters (fol. 12, ܗ), and there are from 20 to 24 lines in each page. This manuscript is written in a good current hand, of the latter part of the ixth cent.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a good current hand, of the latter part of the ixth cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): A later hand has added a note at the foot of fol. 26a.

Contents

This manuscript contains 16 item(s) including nested subsections. N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.
