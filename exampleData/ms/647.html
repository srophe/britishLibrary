Description based on Wright DCCCCLXXIX (3:1151)
Date: 13th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Paper Codex, 23 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Single Lives
This manuscript contains: Serapion. The history of Macarius the Great, composed by Serapion. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 1 item.

Physical Description

Paper, about 9 1/2 in. by 6 1/4, consisting of 23 leaves, several of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 1, 14—18, 22 and 23. The quires, three in number, are signed with letters (ܐ, ܒ, ܓ). Leaves are wanting after foll. 1 and 13. There are from 26 to 35 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a rather inelegant hand of about the xiiith cent.

Physical Description

Paper, about 9 5/8 in. by 6 1/8, consisting of 102 leaves (Add. 14,735, foll. 72—173), some of which are much torn, especially foll. 91—93, 95, 96, 112, and 113. The quires, ten in number, are signed with letters, but the signatures have been altered at various times. There are from 22 to 34 lines in each page. From fol. 156 onwards each page is divided into two columns.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a good, current hand of about the xiith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Foll. 91—94, 97, 104, 105, 114, 121, and 155, are more recent.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Decoration (Illustration): fol. 173 b
