Add. 17,159, foll. 1–63

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/795
Description based on Wright DCXLV (2:511)
Date: 7th century
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 63 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification:
This manuscript contains: Jacob of Batnae. Metrical discourses of Jacob of Batnae; An extract; An extract on the Martyrs; Jacob of Batnae. Part of a discourse onZacchaeusthe publican. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 10 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 7 1/8 in, by 4 5/8, consisting of 63 leaves, some of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 1—3, 12, 17—19, 45, and 46. The quires, signed with letters, are 7 in number, but of these the first and third are very imperfect, several leaves being missing after fol. 1, and single leaves after foll. 12, 16, and 19. There are from 21 to 26 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): The writing is a neat, regular Estrangela of the viith cent., with the exception of foll. 58 b—63 b.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): The extract above is written in a cursive hand of the xth cent.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): The extract on martyrs above is written in a cursive hand of the ixth cent.
Hand 4 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): The discourse on Zacchaeus is written in a hand of the ixth or xth cent.
Hand 5 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.

Add. 17,159, foll. 64–93

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/796
Description based on Wright DCXLVI (2:511-512)
Date: 7th century
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 30 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Individual Authors
This manuscript contains: Jacob of Batnae. Metrical discourses of Jacob of Batnae. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 3 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 7 1/8 in. by 4 5/8, consisting of 30 leaves (Add. 17,159, foll. 64—93), some of which are slightly stained and torn, especially foll. 66 and 86. The quires, signed with letters, were originally at least 8 in number; but the first three are now lost, and there are lacunae after foll. 74 and 85. The number of lines in each page varies from 23 to 26.

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): This manuscript is written in a neat, regular Estrangela of the viith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
