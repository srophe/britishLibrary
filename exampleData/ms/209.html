Add. 14,515

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/209
Description based on Wright CCCVI (1:240-243)
Date: AD 893 / AG 1204
Origin: Convent of S. Mary Deipara
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Codex, 311 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Choral Books
This manuscript contains: Ephraim, Jacob of Batnae, Isaac of Antioch. The first volume of a collection of Choral Services and Homilies for the principal festivals of the whole year. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 69 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 10 1/4 in. by 7 1/8, consisting of 311 leaves, some of which are much stained, defaced, and torn, especially foll. 1—18, 23, 26, 35, 36, 44, 45, and 308—311. The quires, signed with letters, are 30 in number. Leaves are wanting after foll. 11, 14, 16, 18, 35, 108, 113 and 300. Each page is divided into two columns, of from 27 to 40 lines.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This volume is written in a good, current hand, dated A. Gr. 1204, A.D. 893.

Additions

Addition 1 (Fol. 1a): On fol. 1 a there is an index to the discourses contained in this volume, but the leaf is much mutilated.
Addition 2 (Fol. 311b): On fol. 311 b, after the doxology, there is a note, of which the greater part has been torn away . . . ܝܗܝ ܠܓܝܣ̇ܐ. . . ܠܚ̇ܛܝܐ . . . ܩܘܣܢܝܐ . . . ـܘܗܝ . . . ܕܟ̣ܬܒ . . . ܐܡܝܢ..
Addition 3 (Fol. 311b): Another note on the same page, compared with that at the end of Add. 17,190, shows us that this manuscript was written in the convent of S. Mary Deipara, A. Gr. 1204, A.D. 893, for a monk named John, a native of Circesium (ܩܪܩܣܝܘܢ , قَرْقِيسِيا), from the convent of Julian the martyr.
