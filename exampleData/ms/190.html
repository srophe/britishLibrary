Add. 14,500, upper text

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1396
Description based on Wright CCXCIV (1:230)
Date: 11th century
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 89 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Sacerdotals
This manuscript contains: A Sacerdotal; Prayers and sedras. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 8 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 1/4 in. by 5 1/8, consisting of 89 leaves, some of which are slightly stained and torn, especially the first and last. The quires, signed with letters, were originally 10 in number, but the first is lost, and a leaf is wanting after fol. 8. The number of lines in each page varies from 19 to 24. This manuscript is palimpsest throughout (see Add. MS 14,500, lower text ).

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript, which is palimpsest throughout, is written in a good, regular hand of the xith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): The prayers and sedras on foll. 83b-89b are written partly by the writer of the manuscript, and partly by other hands.

Add. 14,500, lower text

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1395
Description based on Wright CCXCIV (1:230)
Date: ca. 7th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Codex, 89 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Sacerdotals
This manuscript contains: Part of a letter addressed to a person in holy orders. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 1 item.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 1/4 in. by 5 1/8, consisting of 89 leaves, some of which are slightly stained and torn, especially the first and last. The whole of this volume is palimpsest (see Add. MS 14,500, upper text ).

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): The whole of this volume is palimpsest, the more ancient text being written in a small, elegant character of about the viith cent, (see, in particular, fol. 5a).

Contents

This manuscript contains 1 item(s). N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.
