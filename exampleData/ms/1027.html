Add. 17,240

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1027
Description based on Wright CCCCXVII (1:326-327)
Date: 13th century
Melkite Syriac script ܀ Paper Codex, 258 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Choral Books
This manuscript contains: The Octôechus of the services of Sunday and of the ferial days, according to the Malkite use.; Lessons from the Epistles and Gospels for the ferial days.; Lessons from the Epistles and Gospels, to be read at the celebration of the holy Eucharist, on Sundays, arranged according to the eight tones.; Lessons for Whitsun Day, to be read at the celebration of the holy Eucharist.; Lessons from the Gospels, to be read at morning prayer, on Sundays.; ... In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 36 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 7 in. by 5 1/8, consisting of 258 leaves, a few of which are slightly stained and torn, especially foll. 1, 8—10, and 252. The quires are 33 in number. From the first to the twenty-sixth, they are marked with Arabic words at the upper corner of the first page (e.g. foll. 19, 27, etc.); the remaining seven are marked with both Arabic words and Syriac letters (from 1 to 7), but have also later Coptic signatures in ciphers (from 27, % to 33, %). Leaves are wanting at the beginning and end, as well as after foll. 4 and 110. There are 14 or 15 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (major, Melkite Syriac script): This volume is written in a neat, regular, Malkite hand of the xiiith cent.
