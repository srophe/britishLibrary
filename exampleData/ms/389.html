Add. 14,635, foll. 1–4

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/390
Description based on Wright DLXV (2:445)
Date: 6th century
Estrangela script ܀ Parchment Other, 4 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Individual Authors
This manuscript contains: Gregory of Nyssa. Fragments of the Commentary of Gregory Nyssen on the Song of Songs. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 3 items.

Physical Description

Four vellum leaves, 13 1/4 in. by 10 1/8, each page of which has three columns, and each column from 49 to 54 lines. The writing is a neat, regular Estrangělā of the vith cent.

Hand 1 (sole, Estrangela script): The writing is a neat, regular Estrangělā of the vith cent.

Add. 14,635, foll. 5–15

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/391
Description based on Wright DLXVIII (2:449-450)
Date: 6th century
Unspecified Syriac script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 11 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Individual Authors
This manuscript contains: Evagrius of Pontus. Fragments of the works of Evagrius. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 10 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 12 1/8 in. by 9 1/2, consisting of 11 leaves (Add. 14,635, foll. 5—15), several of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 5, 10, 11, 14, and 15. There are lacunae after foll. 5, 6, 9, 11, 12, 13, and 14. Each page is divided into 3 columns, of from 36 to 40 lines. The writing is a fine, regular Estrangělā of the vith cent.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): The writing is a fine, regular Estrangělā of the vith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Coptic script): See additions.

Gr. 1205, A.D. 894. ܐܫܬܘܬܦ ܒܕܘܒܩܐ ܗܢܐ ܝܘܚܢܢ ܚܛܝܐ ܘܒܨܝܪܐ ܩܫܝܫܐ ܒܪ ܡܩܪܝ ܪܝܫ ܕܝܪܐ ܕܣܘܪ̈ܝܝܐ ܕܒܡܕܒܪܐ ܕܣܩܝܛܝܣ. ܟܠ ܕܩ̇ܪܐ ܒܗ̣ ܢܨܠܐ ܥܠܘܗܝ ܏ܘܫ. ܟܬܒܬܝ ܗܠܝܢ ܒܫܢܬ ܐܠܦ ܘܡܬܝܢ ܏ܘܗ ♰
Addition 2 (Fol. 5a): Underneath this, the same John has written in Coptic: I pray the God of my fathers, remember my littleness, IJohn the little, priest, son of….Macarius…..(?). May Christ Jesus our Lord forgive me all my sins, because I have laboured at this holy book of Abba Evagrius.

Add. 14,635, foll. 16–18

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/392
Description based on Wright DXLI (2:414)
Date: probably AD 555
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Other, 3 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Individual Authors
This manuscript contains: Ephrem the Syrian. Metrical discourses of Ephraim, 330 in number. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 6 items.

Physical Description

Three vellum leaves, about 13 ¼ in. by 10, all slightly stained and torn (Add. 14,635, foll. 16—18). There are three columns, of from 38 to 42 lines, in each page. The writing is a good, regular Estrangela of the vith cent., the probable date being A.D. 555. If fol. 18 really belongs to the same manuscript as foll. 16 and 17, these leaves are all that are left of a large volume

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): The writing is a good, regular Estrangela of the vith cent., the probable date being A.D.555.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 4 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.

Additions

Addition 1 (Fol. 18a): The first column of fol. 18a has been cut away. The second contains the conclusion of the colophon: ܕܣܝܟܝܢ ܡܐܡܪ̈ܐ ܘܡܕܪ̈ܫܐ ܗܠܝܢ ܟܠܗܘܢ ܕܡܪܝ ܐܦܪܝܡ ܬܠܬܡܐܐ ܘܬܠܬܝܢ. ܟܬܝܒܝܢ ܘܡܦܚܡܝܢ ܒܫܩܠܛܥܢܐ. ܫܘܒܚܐ ܠܐܒܐ ܏ܘܫ.
Addition 2 (Fol. 18a): Then follows a note, written in a smaller and more cursive character, stating that this manuscript was purchased by one Alexander, a monk of the convent of Sergius at —, A. Gr.866, A.D.555. ܢܗܘܐ ܕܘܟܪܢܐ ܛܒܐ ܩܕܡ ܐܠܗܐ ܘܡܫܝܚܗ ܘܪܘܚܗ ܚܝܐ ܘܩܕܝܫܐ ܠܒܨܝܪܐ ܐܠܟܣܢܕܪܐ ܕܥܘܡܪܐ ܩܕܝܫܐ ܕܒܝܬ ܡܪܝ ܣܪܓܝܣ . . . ܐ ܕܐܬܚܦܛ ܘܙܒܢ ܠܗ ܦܢܩܝܬܐ ܗܕܐ ܕܡܕܪ̈ܫܐ ܕܛܘܒܢܐ ܡܪܝ ܐܦܪܝܡ ܡܢ ܕܝܠܗ ܘܡܢ ܡܪ̈ܗܛܘܗܝ ܠܦܘܪܩܢܐ ܕܚ̈ܝܘܗܝ ܘܠܝܘܬܪܢܐ ܕܚܒ̈ܝܒܘܗܝ. ܏ܘܫ. ܙܒܝܢܐ ܕܝܢ ܦܢܩܝܬܐ ܗܕܐ ܫܢܬ ܬܡܢܡܐܐ ܘܫܬܝܢ ܘܫܬ ܒܐܝܪܚ ܟܢܘܢ ܩܕܝܡ.
Addition 3 (Fol. 18a): The third column of the same page contains two notes, the first of which records the presentation of the book to the convent of — by the priest —, of the convent of Naphshāthā, in the time of the abbat Thomas. ܐܫܬܟܢ ܘܐܝܬܘܗܝ ܟܬܒܐ ܗܢܐ ܝܘܡܢܐ ܕܥܘܡܪܐ ܩܕܝܫܐ . . . ܕܐܫܬܟܢ ܡܢ ܡܢܚ ܢܦܫܐ ܩܫܝܫܐ . . . ܕܕܝܪܐ ܕܢܦܫ̈ܬܐ ܒܝ̈ܘܡܘܗܝ ܕܪܚܡ ܐܠܗܐ ܩܫܝܫܐܪܝܫܕܝܪܐ ܡܪܝ ܬܐܘܡܐ ܕܝܠܗ ܕܕܝܪܐ. ܏ܘܫ.
Addition 4 (Fol. 18a): The other note is not quite so legible, but it shows that the book belonged to the convent of S. Mary Deipara in the time of the abbat Salībā, when Abraham (Ephraim) was patriarch of Alexandria (i.e. A.D.977-981; See Renaudot, Hist. patr. Jacob. Alexandr., p. 366, and Le Quien, Or. Christ., t. ii. col. 476). ܐܝܬܘܗܝ ܟܬܒܐ ܕܡܕܪ̈ܫܐ ܕܩܕܝܫܐܡܪܝ ܐܦܪܝܡ ܡܠܦܢܐ . . . ܐܒ̇݅ܪܗܡܦܛܝܪܟܝܣ ܒܝܘ̈ܡܝ ܨܠܝܒܐ ܪܝܫܕܝܪܐ ܕܒܡܕܒܪܐ ܕܣܩܝܛܐ. ܟܠ ܕܡܦܩ ܠܗ ܡܢ ܡܕܒܪܐܬܚܬܚܪܡܐ ܐܝܬܘܗܝ ܐܡܝܢ.

Date: 11th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Other, 4 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Lectionaries
This manuscript contains: Lessons from the Gospels for Passion Holy Week. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 5 items.

Physical Description

Four vellum leaves, about 12 5/8 in. by 10, all slightly stained and torn (Add. 14,635, foll. 21—24). Each page is divided into two columns, of from 26 to 30 lines. There is a lacuna between foll. 22 and 23. The writing is a stiff, regular Estrangela, probably of the xith cent.
