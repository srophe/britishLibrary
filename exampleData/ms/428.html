Add. 14,660

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/428
Description based on Wright DCCCCLXXXVIII (3:1160-1162)
Date: 9th or 10th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Codex, 81 leaves.
Features: Decoration
Wright's Subject Classification: Logic and Rhetoric
This manuscript contains: Probus. The commentary of Probus on the περὶ ἑρμηνείας of Aristotle; Severus Sabocht. A treatise by Severus Sabocht, bishop of Ḳinnesrīn , on the Syllogisms in the Analytica Priora of Aristotle; Severus Sabocht. A letter of Severus Sabocht to the priest Aitīlāhā on certain terms in the treatise περὶ ἑρμηνείας; Paul the Persian. A discourse of Paul the Persian on the art of Logic, addressed to Khusrau Nūshīrwān, king of Persia; An anonymous Isagoge, or Introduction to the art of Logic, translated from the Greek by the monk Athanasius, of the convent of Malchus; .... In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 6 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 11 1/4 in. by 7 1/4, consisting of 81 leaves, the last of which is much soiled and torn. The quires, signed with letters, were originally 11 in number (ܙ — ܐ and ܕ — ܐ), but the first is lost, and several others are imperfect. Two leaves are wanting after fol. 14, no less than eight after fol. 19, and a single leaf after fol. 61. There are from 28 to 36 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This volume is written in a good, regular hand of the ixth or xth cent.
Decoration (Rubrication): Probus' commentary on Aristotle's περὶ ἑρμηνείας . The text is distinguished from the commentary by the letters ܐ and ܦ in red ink.
