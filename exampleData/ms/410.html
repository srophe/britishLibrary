Add. 14,646, foll. 1–133

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/411
Description based on Wright DCCCCXXXVII (3:1086-1087)
Date: 6th century
Estrangela script ܀ Parchment Codex, 133 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Collected Lives
This manuscript contains: Athanasius. The life ofAntonybyAthanasius; Palladius. Copious extracts from the lives of the Egyptian Solitaries by Palladius. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 14 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 1/2 in. by 5 1/4, consisting of 133 leaves, some of which are slightly stained and torn, especially foll. 1—10, 28, and 99. The quires, now signed with letters, are 15 in number, but one at least is wanting at the end. There are from 20 to 28 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (sole, Estrangela script): This volume is written in a fine, regular Estrangela of the vith cent.

Add. 14,646, foll. 134–194, upper text

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/412
Description based on Wright DCCCCXXXIX (3:1087)
Date: 6th century
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 61 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Collected Lives
This manuscript contains: The life ofSerapion; The history ofPaul the bishopandJohn the priest. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 2 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 3/8 in. by 5 1/4, consisting of 61 leaves (Add, 14,646, foll. 134—194) a few of which are slightly stained and torn, especially foll. 134—141, 174—177, and 194. The quires, six in number, were originally signed with arithmetical figures (e.g. fol. 163 a, ��); but later hands have marked them with letters, from ܐ to ܘ and from ܝܘ to ܟܐ. There are from 24 to 30 lines in each page. Foll. 183—194 are palimpsest (see Add. 14,646 foll. 183-194, lower text ).

The original description of this manuscript by William Wright includes characters, symbols, or illustrations which cannot be represented. Please consult the attached image, vol. 3, p. 1087.

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): This manuscript is written in a fine, regular Estrangela of the vith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): foll. 183—194 are in a current hand of about the xth cent.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Later hands have marked the quires with letters, from ܐ to ܘ and from ܝܘ to ܟܐ.
