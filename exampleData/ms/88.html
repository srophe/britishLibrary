Add. 14,432, foll. 1–120, 122

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/1173
Date: 6th century
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 121 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Old Testament
This manuscript contains: Simeon the son of ܪܓܝܘ. An index of lessons; The book of Isaiah, according to the Peshitta version. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 2 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 1/2 in. by 5 3/4, consisting of 121 leaves, some of which are slightly stained and torn, especially foll. 1—5 and 122. The quires, 15 in number, seem originally to have been left unsigned; they are now signed with letters, in one or two instances incorrectly. Each page has from 19 to 22 lines.

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): The writing is a fine, regular Estrangela of the vith cent.
Hand 2 (minor, West Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Simeon the son of ܪܓܝܘ, who drew up the index of lessons. See additions.
Hand 4 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 5 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 6 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 7 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.

Contents

This manuscript contains 2 item(s). N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 1b): Simeon the son of ܪܓܝܘ . An index of lessons
 
Title: ܦܘܪܫ ܩܪ̈ܝܢܐ ܕܐܫܥܝܐ ܢܒܝܐ ܡ̇ܫܒܚܐ ܕܢܒܝ̈ܐ.
Item 2 (Fol. 3b): The book of Isaiah, according to the Peshitta version.
 
Title: ܢܒܝܘܬܗ ܕܐܫܥܝܐ
Note: Some lessons are indicated by rubrics in the text.

Additions

N.B., folio location may not be available for some items below.
N.B., items below may have been listed out of folio order in Wright's catalogue.
Addition 1 (Fol. 122a): What originally stood after the doxology, fol. 122a, has been erased, in order to make room for a note in a modern hand. Of this latter only a few words are now legible, owing to the lower part of the leaf being torn away, but it evidently contained the name of a possessor of the volume: ܐܝܬܘܗܝ ܟܬܒܐ ܗܢܐ ܕܐܫܥ[ܝܐ] . . . ܩܫܝܫ[ܐ] . . .
Addition 2 (Fol. 122b): On fol. 122b there is a note of older date, probably of the xth cent., stating that the manuscript belonged to Leontius, abbat of the convent of Mār Theodore on the mountain to the west of Sarīn , who, with his disciple Constantine, purchased it for the use of the convent: ܢܗܘܐ ܕܘܟܪܢܐ ܛܒܐ ܩܕܡ ܐܠܗܐ ܠܩܫܝܫܐ ܡܪܝ ܠܐܘܢܛܝ ܕܒܪ̈ܚ̣ܡܘܗܝ ܕܐܠܗܐ ܪܝܫܕܝܪܐ ܕܥܘܡܪܐ ܩܕܝܫܐ ܕܡܪܝ ܬܐܕܘܪܐ ܕܛܘܪܐ ܕܡܥܪ̈ܒܝ ܣܪܝܢ܇ ܕܡܢ ܥܡܠܐ ܕܐܝܼܕ̈ܘܗܝ ܘܡܢ ܕܘܥܬܐ ܕܐܦ̈ܘܗܝ̇. ܘܕܩܘܣܛ ܬܠܡܝܕܗ. ܐܬܚ̇ܦܛܘܢ ܘܣܡ̣ܘܢ ܣܝܼܡܬܐ ܗܕܐ ܒܥܘܡܪܗܘܿܢ. ܡܛܠ ܥܘܗܕܢܐ ܕܥܢܝ̈ܕܝܗܘܢ ܘܢܘܛ̇ܪܐ ܕܚܝܝ̈ܗܘܢ. ܕܐܠܗܐ ܗ̇ܘ ܕܡܛܠܬܗ ܥܒ̣ܕܘܢ ܗܕܐ̣. ܗܼܘ ܢܫܘܐ ܐܢܘܢ ܘ܏ܫ.
Addition 3 (Fol. 3a): The above note is in the handwriting of one Simeon the son of ܪܓܝܘ, the same who drew up the index of lessons, as may be seen from the note appended to that index, fol. 3a: ܡܛܠ ܚܘܒܗ ܘܚ̈ܫܘܗܝ ܩ̈ܕܝܫܐ ܕܡܫܝܚܐ ܐܠܗܐ ܦܪܘܩܢ: ܟܠ ܕܡ̇ܦܪܫ ܩܪ̈ܝܢܐ ܒܟܬܒܐ ܗܢܐ ܕܛܘܒܬܢܐ ܐܫܥܝܐ̣ ܢܨ̇ܠܐ ܥܠ ܚܛܝܐ ܘܕܘ̇ܝܐ . . . ܫܡܥܘܢ ܒܪ ܪܓܝܘ ܕܣ̣ܡ ܒܗ ܩܪ̈ܝܢܐ̣. ܘܥܠ ܩܫܝܫܐ ܡܪܝ ܠܐܘܢܛܝ ܡܪܗ ܕܝܠܗ ܕܟܬܒܐ ܏܏ܘܫ
Addition 4 (Fol. 1a): The large writing that once covered fol. 1a has been partially effaced, and over it there is now written a prayer of Jacob of Batnae, ܒܥܘܬܐ ܕܡܪܝ ܝܥܩܘܒ, beginning: ܘܝ ܚܛܝܐ ܐܝܟܐ ܣܚܝܦ ܐܢܬ ܡܐ ܕܚ̇ܛܐ ܐܢܬ: ܏ܘܫ
Addition 5: The quires are now signed with letters, in one or two instances incorrectly.
Addition 6: A few Greek vowels have been subsequently added ().
Addition 7: Many more lessons have been added on the margins by a later hand, which has often altered the original rubrics.

Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Parchment Leaf, 1 leaf.
Wright's Subject Classification: Old Testament
This manuscript contains: Jeremiah, ch. i. 5—8, ch. ix. 23, 24, etc. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 1 item.

Physical Description

The leaf, or rather scrap of vellum, numbered fol. 121, does not belong to this volume , but is written in a comparatively modern hand, with many abbreviations.

Hand 1 (major, Unspecified Syriac script): Written in a comparatively modern hand
