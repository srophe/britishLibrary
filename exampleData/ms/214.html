Add. 14,520

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/214
Description based on Wright CCCCLI (1:363-365)
Date: 8th or 9th century
Estrangela script ܀ Parchment Codex, 161 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Hymns
This manuscript contains: Hymns; A collection of hymns (ܡܕܪ̈ܫܐ, ܡܥ̈ܢܝܬܐ, etc.), prayers, lessons and services for various occasions. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 55 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 9 in. by 5 7/8, consisting of 161 leaves, the first three of which are much stained and torn. The quires were originally 20 in number, but the last two are now lost. They are signed with Syriac letters on the first leaf of each, and by a later hand with Greek letters on the last leaf. The number of lines in each page varies from 26 to 31.

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): This volume is written in a clear, though rather inelegant Estrangela of the viiith or ixth cent.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): The hymns on foll. 2, 3, and 4 a are in a more cursive character, but probably of the same age, perhaps written in part by the same hand, as the rest of the manuscript. Compare the marginal notes on foll. 145 b and 151 a.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
