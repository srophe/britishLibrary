Add. 14,730, foll. 1–111

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/636
Description based on Wright DCCCXXXIV (2:865-866)
Date: 12th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Paper Codex, 111 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Collected Authors
This manuscript contains: Palladius, Palladius. Part of the Paradise of Palladius; Isaiah of Scete. An extract from Isaiah of Scete on humility; Palladius, Palladius. An extract from the sixth chapter of the Paradise of Palladius; Palladius. The remainder of the Paradise, from the middle of chap. 5 to the end; Part of a discourse in the metre of Jacob of Batnae , on the Crucifixion of our Lord and the Dispersion of the Apostles to different countries; .... In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 10 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 7 3/8 in. by 5 1/4, consisting of 111 leaves, some of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 1, 57, 65, 82, 91, 92, 102, and 103. The quires are signed with letters from ܐ to ܝܚ, but several are missing and others are imperfect, there being lacunae after foll. 9, 15, 26, 36, 46, 63, 73, 81, 82, 84, 92, and 111. Each page is divided into two columns, as far as fol. 26. The number of lines varies from 17 to 20.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in an inelegant, current hand of the xiith cent.

Add. 14,730, foll. 112–164

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/637
Description based on Wright DCCCCLIX (3:1122-1123)
Date: 12th century
Unspecified Syriac script ܀ Paper Codex, 53 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: Collected Lives
This manuscript contains: The history of Theophilus and Maria; John of Asia. An excerpt from the history of Susanna by John of Asia; John of Asia. An excerpt from the history of Ḥarpaṭ by John of Asia; The history of Paul the solitary, of the Thebaid; The history of John of Rome; .... In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 15 items.

Physical Description

Paper, about 7 3/8 in. by 5 3/8, consisting of 53 leaves (Add. 14,730, foll. 112—164), some of which are much stained and torn, especially foll. 150-152, 157, 158, and 164. The quires are signed with letters, the remaining signatures being ܝܗ-ܝܚ. Leaves are wanting at the beginning, as well as after foll. 119, 149, 151, 157, and 163. There are from 16 to 18 lines in each page.

Hand 1 (sole, Unspecified Syriac script): This manuscript is written in a rather inelegant hand of the xiith cent.
