Thirteen vellum leaves, about 11 in. by 8, several of which are much stained and torn, and two are mere fragments. From 32 to 41 lines.

Contents

This manuscript contains 1 item(s). N.B. Items were re-numbered for this online catalog and may not reflect William Wright's previous numeration.

Item 1 (Fol. 2a): Eusebius. Portions of a Chronicle, based on that of Eusebius, with a continuation by the compiler. [defective]
Notes:
  • The part extant commences with an account of the composition of the Septuagint Version of the Scriptures, and extends, in a fragmentary condition, down to A. Gr. 975 (ܬܬܩܥܗ), A.D. 664
  • The fly-leaf fol. 1 of the St. Petersburg manuscript of the Ecclesiastical History of Eusebius originally belonged to this manuscript. It comprises the reigns of Ptolemy Lagus and Ptolemy Philadelphus, some account of the family of the Herods, and part of the history of our Lord.
