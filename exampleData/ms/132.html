Add. 14,459, foll. 1–66

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/133
Description based on Wright XC (1:64)
Date: 5th century
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 66 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: New Testament
This manuscript contains: The first two Gospels, according to the Peshitta version. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 3 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 in. by 4 7/8, consisting of 66 leaves, the first three of which are slightly stained. The quires were 8 in number, but the first is now lost. They seem to have been originally signed with arithmetical figures (though the ciphers on foll. 1a and 12a are more recent), but the present signatures are letters. There are from 27 to 31 lines in each page. This manuscript is written in a beautiful, Edessene Estrangela, apparently of the vthcent.; with the exception of fol. 12, which was probably added by the same person who retouched foll. 1—3.

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): This manuscript is written in a beautiful, Edessene Estrangela, apparently of the 5th century.
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Perhaps of the 10th century. See additions.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 4 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.

Add. 14,459, foll. 67–169

URI: https://bl.syriac.uk/ms/134
Description based on Wright CI (1:67-68)
Date: ca. AD 530-540 / AG 84..
Estrangela script (multiple hands) ܀ Parchment Codex, 103 leaves.
Wright's Subject Classification: New Testament
This manuscript contains: The Last Two Gospels, according to the Peshitta version. In total, including sub-sections, this manuscript contains 3 items.

Physical Description

Vellum, about 8 in. by 5, consisting of 103 leaves, the last 8 of which are much stained and soiled (Add. 14,459, foll. 67—169). The quires, 11 in number, seem to have been originally signed with arithmetical figures (though the ciphers on fol. 67 aare more recent), but the present signatures are letters (from ܛto ܝܛ). There are from 25 to 27 lines in each page. The writing is a small, elegant Estrangela of the vith cent, (between A.D. 530 and 540). Fol. 74 is a palimpsest leaf, probably added by the same person who retouched foll. 162 and 163.

Hand 1 (major, Estrangela script): The writing is a small, elegant Estrangela of the 6th cent, (between A.D. 530 and 540).
Hand 2 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): Of the 9th or 10th century. See additions.
Hand 3 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 4 (minor, Unspecified Greek script): See additions.
Hand 5 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See additions.
Hand 6 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See decoration notes.
Hand 7 (minor, Unspecified Syriac script): See decoration notes.
Decoration (Illustration): On fol. 67 a there is an ornamental Cross, containing the words ܚܘܪ ܠܘܬܗ ܘܣܒܪ ܒܗ ܨܠܝܒܐ ܚܝܐ in a comparatively modern hand.
Decoration (Ornamentation): Coloured ornament on fol. 169b.

Additions

N.B., folio location may not be available for some items below.
Addition 1: The ciphers on fol. 67 a are more recent. The present signatures are letters (from ܛ to ܝܛ).
Addition 2: Many lessons have been noted on the margins by later hands, sometimes in barbarous Greek (e. g. foll.94 a, 105 b, 107 b, 110 b).
Addition 3: Fol. 74 is a palimpsest leaf, probably added by the same person who retouched foll.162 and 163.Fol. 74 is a palimpsest leaf of the 9th or 10th century, the more ancient text being that of S. Matthew.
Addition 4 (Fol. 169b): On fol. 169 b we have the ordinary doxology; and under it, at some distance below the coloured ornament, there is a note, much injured and effaced, containing the date: ܐܫܬܠܡ ܟܬܒܐ ܗܢܐ ܒܝܪܚ . . . ܐܡܢܡܐܐ ܘܐܪ̈ܒܥܝܢ . . . “This book was finished in the month of . . . ,(in the year) 84 . . (of the Greeks), i. e.A.D. 53 . . . From the remaining lines of the note it can be gathered that this manuscript was written at some person's expense, as a present to a church or monastery.
Addition 5 (Fol. 169b): Of the later writing, with which the greater part of this page is covered, but little is distinctly legible. The name of a village called Kěphar-Těkīrīn, ܩܪܝܬܐ ܪܚܡܬ ܠܡܫܝܚܐ ܟܦܪ ܬܟܝܪܝܢ occurs several times.
